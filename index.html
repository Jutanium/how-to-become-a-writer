by Dan Jutan

The question comes amid some more ordinary ones.

When's your next class?

Are you going to the career fair?

What'd you get on that last exam?
Sometimes the ordinary questions are enough to drag you through the sand of your daily life.
Sometimes the sand is quicksand, and the ordinary not quick enough.
Sometimes you want to ask questions that matter.
What is your story?
Who are you and how do you know?
How do they know?
How do you tell them?
no matter who you are, you have something meaningful to say about writing.
What does it mean to call yourself a writer?
Does it mean that you write a lot?
Does it mean that you write well, that you write something worth giving you the title?
Does someone else have to give you the title, or can you give it to yourself?
You imagine walking into an empty field filled with your past accomplishments.
You look up at a set of empty bleachers, and shout, "Writer!"
You don't score any points that game.
You’re probably not a writer. You don’t suspect anyone else would call you one.
Could you call yourself a writer? Nobody would have to know . . .
It is not pride in the title nor affinity to writing that attracts you to the idea of calling yourself by this new name.
It’s the certainty that a title provides. If you are a writer, then you are something, and you know what it is.
You can tell it to your advisor or your parents or your date. Your date finds it interesting. You find it interesting.
Now you can keep a folder full of fragments.
Little pieces
of your life, which is now worth keeping in a folder.
Now that you’re a writer, you have a daily task. You have something to tell yourself when you go to sleep at night. You have a goal, and it doesn’t depend on your last exam score. If you do it right, it might not even depend on the career fair.
Anxiety kicks in. You realize your dreaming went too far. Slow down.
You had determined that you could call yourself a writer.
You could call, you could call the shots.
Maybe, instead of “writer” becoming who you are, you let writing reflect what you already are.
